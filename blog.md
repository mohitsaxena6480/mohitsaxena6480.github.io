## 🧘 Post 3: Koshas: FIVE Bodies

### Introduction
There is more to a Human Body than our current perception tells us. According to Yogic Sciences, human body consists of five bodies or sheaths known as **Koshas**.

![Koshas: Five Bodies](https://i0.wp.com/scienceofyoga.org.in/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/sheaths-4.png)

### ANNAMAYA KOSHA
The first sheath is the Physical Body or the **Annamaya Kosha** which is our skin, bones, joints, liver, kidney, and a lot more.

Anna in Sanskrit means Food, so Annamaya Kosha is the Food Body. It is a product of all the nourishment that a person has ingested over the years.

### PRANAMAYA KOSHA
The second sheath is the Energy Body or the **Pranamaya Kosha** in which **Prana**, or the Life Energy flows in Energy Channels or Nadis. It is this life energy or **Prana** which powers our Physical & Mental bodies.

Human Body has approximately 72000 Nadis have energy vortices or Junction Points also known as Chakras.

### MANOMAYA KOSHA
The third sheath is the Mental Body or the **Manomaya Kosha** which directs our thoughts, emotions, memories, decisions, and intellect.

In Yoga, Mind is not just the brain, instead, every cell in the human body has its own intelligence. And, there is an entire Mental Body.

### VIGNANAMAYA KOSHA
The above three layers are all physical in nature differing only in the subtlety of our perception experienced through our five senses.

However, there is a fourth layer called the Wisdom Sheath or the **Vignanamaya Kosha**. It is a transient body between the first three physical sheaths and the last non-physical sheath and is beyond the sensory perceptions.

The real meaning of this sheath is **Vishesh Gyana** or knowledge which cannot be perceived through sense organs.

Once accessed it opens doors to the unlimited knowledge of the universe. Modern Science is just bits and pieces of this unlimited knowledge.

### ANANDAMAYA KOSHA
The last and the fifth sheath is the Bliss Body or the **Anandamaya Kosha** which has nothing to do with physical realms of life.

This nonphysical body cannot be described or even defined in words and can only be experienced.

Once a person has access to this body, it produces an overwhelming experience of bliss. The impact of time (of now & then) and space (of here & there) are transcended upon experience of this body.

## 🧘 Post 5: Prana: The life Energy

### Prana and its Qualities

### How Human Body gets Prana?

#### Externally
Externally the requirement for Prana is fulfilled from the environment through food, air, water and sun.

Breathing in air not only provides vital oxygen for metabolic processes but also a certain quantity of Prana.

Visiting a mountain gives a feeling of solitude and tranquility. This is because air is high in Prana at high altitudes.

This is a proven fact in the laboratory that Sattvic diet is high in Pranic value while Rajasic & Tamasic diet is low in Pranic value. Sattvic food is described as diet containing Fruits, raw vegetables, sea fish etc. Rajasic & Tamasic food contains processed food, nonvegetarian including meat, eggs, poultry etc.

#### Internally
Each individual has a certain quantum of Prana within himself. The quantum of Prana changes continuously both quantitatively & qualitatively as per the activities of an individual.

Positive thoughts & emotions and Yogic Practices of Pranayama tend to generate higher levels of Prana in the body intrinsically.

The quantum of Prana as well as its quality has a subtle reflection on every aspect of human life. The dynamism that one sees in a persons personality is all because of enhanced level of Prana.

### Why understanding science of Prana is Important?
Different manifestations of [Consciousness & Energy](https://scienceofyoga.org.in/macrocosm-and-microcosm/) are present in every aspect of manifested creation.

Whether it is budding of a flower, blowing of a wind, running of a car, flowing of water or movement of a human body, everything is because of **Prana or Life Energy**.

Understanding the science of Prana is important because the 'Rise in Human Consciousness' is facilitated by the movement of Prana across different [Koshas](https://scienceofyoga.org.in/the-five-bodies/) of the body.

## 🧘 Post 15: What is Meditation?

### Hatha Yoga vs Meditation
Yoga is derived from the root Sanskrit Word, YUJ, which means Union. The real goal of Yoga is to bring harmony between mind & body, prompting the union of Individual Consciousness with the Universal Consciousness.

Patanjali's Ashtanga Yoga consists of Eight Limbs: **YAMA, NIYAMA, ASANAS, PRANAYAMA, PRATYAHARA, DHARANA, DHYANA, SAMADHI**.

The real aim of the science of Hatha Yoga is absolute purification of the entire body to prepare for advanced practices.

### Pratyahara, Dharana & Dhyana
Dhyana, the 7th limb of Ashtanga Yoga, is the Real Meditation. It almost always begins in conjunction with the previous two steps of Pratyahara (Withdrawal of Senses) and Dharana (Focusing on the Object of Concentration).

A person becomes Meditative when one is in complete oneness with the object of concentration. Samadhi, the 8th limb, is the ultimate goal of Yoga—uniting Individual Consciousness with Universal Consciousness.
