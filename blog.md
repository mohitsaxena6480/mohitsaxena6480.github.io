## 🧘 Post 3: Koshas: FIVE Bodies

### Introduction
There is more to a Human Body than our current perception tells us[cite: 3]. According to Yogic Sciences, human body consists of five bodies or sheaths known as **Koshas**[cite: 3].

![Koshas: Five Bodies](https://i0.wp.com/scienceofyoga.org.in/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/sheaths-4.png)[cite: 3]

### ANNAMAYA KOSHA
The first sheath is the Physical Body or the **Annamaya Kosha** which is our skin, bones, joints, liver, kidney, and a lot more[cite: 3].

Anna in Sanskrit means Food, so Annamaya Kosha is the Food Body[cite: 3]. It is a product of all the nourishment that a person has ingested over the years[cite: 3].

The second sheath is the Energy Body or the **Pranamaya Kosha** in which **Prana**, or the Life Energy flows in Energy Channels or Nadis[cite: 3]. It is this life energy or **Prana** which powers our Physical & Mental bodies[cite: 3].

Human Body has approximately 72000 Nadis have energy vortices or Junction Points also known as Chakras[cite: 3].

### MANOMAYA KOSHA
The third sheath is the Mental Body or the **Manomaya Kosha** which directs our thoughts, emotions, memories, decisions, and intellect[cite: 3].

In Yoga, Mind is not just the brain, instead, every cell in the human body has its own intelligence[cite: 3]. And, there is an entire Mental Body[cite: 3].

### VIGNANAMAYA KOSHA
The above three layers are all physical in nature differing only in the subtlety of our perception experienced through our five senses[cite: 3].

However, there is a fourth layer called the Wisdom Sheath or the **Vignanamaya Kosha**[cite: 3]. It is a transient body between the first three physical sheaths and the last non-physical sheath and is beyond the sensory perceptions[cite: 3].

The real meaning of this sheath is **Vishesh Gyana** or knowledge which cannot be perceived through sense organs[cite: 3].

Once accessed it opens doors to the unlimited knowledge of the universe[cite: 3]. Modern Science is just bits and pieces of this unlimited knowledge[cite: 3].

### ANANDAMAYA KOSHA
The last and the fifth sheath is the Bliss Body or the **Anandamaya Kosha** which has nothing to do with physical realms of life[cite: 3].

This nonphysical body cannot be described or even defined in words and can only be experienced[cite: 3].

Once a person has access to this body, it produces an overwhelming experience of bliss[cite: 3]. The impact of time (of now & then) and space (of here & there) are transcended upon experience of this body[cite: 3].

Different manifestations of [Consciousness & Energy](https://scienceofyoga.org.in/macrocosm-and-microcosm/) are present in every aspect of manifested creation[cite: 3].

Whether it is budding of a flower, blowing of a wind, running of a car, flowing of water or movement of a human body, everything is because of **Prana or Life Energy**[cite: 3].

Understanding the science of Prana is important because the 'Rise in Human Consciousness' is facilitated by the movement of Prana across different [Koshas](https://scienceofyoga.org.in/the-five-bodies/) of the body[cite: 3].

## 🧘 Post 15: What is Meditation?

The real aim of the science of Hatha Yoga is absolute purification of the entire body to prepare for advanced practices[cite: 3].

### Pratyahara, Dharana & Dhyana
Dhyana, the 7th limb of Ashtanga Yoga, is the Real Meditation[cite: 3]. It almost always begins in conjunction with the previous two steps of Pratyahara (Withdrawal of Senses) and Dharana (Focusing on the Object of Concentration)[cite: 3].

A person becomes Meditative when one is in complete oneness with the object of concentration[cite: 3]. Samadhi, the 8th limb, is the ultimate goal of Yoga—uniting Individual Consciousness with Universal Consciousness[cite: 3].
